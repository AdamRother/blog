## The three-layer architecture

This is the core product structure. Use these explanations in copy — they are written
for non-technical readers.

### Layer 1: The Brain
"Every framework. Every process. Every SOP. Every decision-making pattern you've developed
over years of doing the work. Loaded once, applied everywhere."

"Right now, all of that lives in your head. Maybe some of it is written down somewhere.
Most of it isn't."

"The Brain is where you externalize everything you know. And for the first time, you can
actually see how your business thinks."

Without the Brain: generic AI responses. Smart, but not yours.
With the Brain: every output runs through your methodology. Your way of thinking. Your voice. Your standards.

In copy: "The Brain holds who you are."

### Layer 2: Workspaces
"When you load a client, they get a completely isolated workspace. Their own data. Their own
files. Their own projects. Their own conversation history. Nothing leaks between clients. Ever."

"Your Brain — your methodology, your frameworks, your way of thinking — flows into every
workspace automatically."

In copy: "Workspaces hold who your clients are."

Note: The product feature is called "Workspaces." The live page still says "Client Workspaces"
in some places, but the brand-approved term as of May 2026 is "Workspaces."

### Layer 3: Intelligence Mode
"Intelligence mode sits above your workspaces and gives you a single place to manage everything.
It can think across your entire business."

Example questions Intelligence Mode answers:
- "Which clients need my attention this week?"
- "What patterns are working across my best-performing clients?"
- "Where are the gaps in my delivery right now?"

In copy: "Intelligence connects them."

**The simple version for copy:**
"The Brain holds who you are. Workspaces hold who your clients are. Intelligence connects
them. Three layers, one operating system."

## Scope by design (team seats)

For posts about team management or scaling with staff:

Team seats can work inside scoped clients and projects while the business brain stays
controlled, reviewed, and clean.

How it works:
- A team member's input becomes a "suggestion" — not automatic business memory
- The owner reviews, accepts, rejects, or edits before it affects future work
- No cross-client leakage: access is scoped before the AI answers, and durable memory is
  confirmed before it affects future work

"Powerful without becoming leaky."

## Features — what is actually built

Use this list for accuracy. Do not claim features not listed here.

**Core platform:**
- Three-layer architecture (Brain, Intelligence Mode, Workspaces)
- Voice-first interaction (Whisper transcription + Grok voice TTS)
- Executive Mode pipeline (every message goes straight to Claude Opus)
- 27 Intelligence tools for cross-client operations
- 16 action/output skills
- Streaming responses with markdown rendering
- Deep Research (Perplexity integration)
- Brain Dump mode (voice-first knowledge capture)
- Save as Document, Context, or Framework from chat
- Session history with retry

**Knowledge system:**
- Master Frameworks library
- Account Context (always-on knowledge)
- Instructions (pushed to workspaces automatically)
- Memory Facts (auto-extracted from conversations; user-facing term is "facts", not "memories")
- Per-workspace Frameworks, Context, and Documents
- Folder system with inline TipTap editor
- Bulk import: Google Drive, Notion, GitHub, direct upload
- Two-phase document processing (instant save + background AI analysis)
- Hybrid search with Cohere reranking

**Client management:**
- Client creation, grid/list views
- 80+ field client profiles
- Projects with task management
- Workflows with templates
- Drag-and-drop, favorites, sorting, tags

**Integrations:**
- Google Drive, Google Calendar, Gmail
- Notion, Slack, GitHub, Zoom
- Circle SSO (community)

**Security:**
- Full data isolation between workspaces
- Row-level security on every table
- Encrypted data at rest
- Never used to train external models
- "Your data is encrypted, isolated per client, and never used to train external models."
