## The three-layer architecture

This is the core product structure. Use these explanations in copy — they are written
for non-technical readers.

### Layer 1: The Brain
Your methodology, visible and alive. Every framework, process, SOP, and decision-making
pattern you've developed over years. Loaded once, applied everywhere. For the first time,
you can see how your business thinks.

In copy: "The Brain holds who you are."

### Layer 2: Workspaces
Every client gets their own protected world. Completely isolated. Their own data, files,
projects, conversation history. Nothing leaks between clients. Your Brain flows into every
workspace automatically — your methodology applied in every client's context.

In copy: "Workspaces hold who your clients are."

### Layer 3: Intelligence Mode
Sits above workspaces. Single place to manage everything. Lets you think across your entire
business. "Which clients need attention?" "What patterns are working?" "Where are the gaps?"

In copy: "Intelligence connects them."

**The simple version for copy:**
"The Brain holds who you are. Workspaces hold who your clients are. Intelligence connects
them. Three layers, one operating system."

## The five differentiators

Use these when explaining what makes CI structurally different from generic AI tools.
These are not feature claims — they are architectural facts.

1. **One centralized brain for all your IP.** Frameworks, processes, methodology, personality,
   decision patterns — all in one place. Loaded once, applied everywhere.

2. **Isolated workspaces.** Each client's data completely separated. No leakage between clients.
   Compliant, secure, auditable. Cannot be replicated by organizing folders in Claude or ChatGPT.

3. **Brain knowledge applied everywhere.** Your IP flows into every workspace in a
   context-aware, custom-tailored way. Generic AI cannot do this — it does not understand
   the relationship between your methodology and a specific client's situation.

4. **Voice-first interaction.** Training, strategizing, managing — all by voice. When people
   type, they filter. When they talk, they brain dump. They reveal tone, temperament,
   frameworks they don't even know they have. The more someone talks to CI, the smarter it gets.

5. **It trains on you.** Every interaction makes it smarter. Learns your voice, patterns,
   preferences. Over time, it thinks like you. The accumulated intelligence cannot be exported
   to a generic tool — that is the moat.

## Who it is for (ICP)

**Primary:** Service providers who work with multiple clients. Sub-$3M revenue.

**Specific verticals:** Marketing agencies, sales consultants, AI consultants, growth coaches,
online educators, freelancers with clients, financial advisors, roofers — any service business
with multiple clients.

**Profile:**
- Has 5-50+ clients they're actively managing
- Not technical. Knows they should use AI but hasn't systematized it
- Overwhelmed by complexity. Maybe using Claude or ChatGPT casually but not structurally
- Often on their phone, driving, between meetings — needs mobile
- Has proprietary frameworks and processes (even if they don't call them that)
- Somewhere between $500K and $3M/year

**Identity ratio in messaging:** 65% speaks to who it's for. 35% creates contrast with
who it's not for. "This is not for everyone" is a feature, not a disclaimer.

## What they're doing now (the enemy)

This is the status quo CI replaces. Use this framing when describing the problem:

Five platforms duct-taped together. Claude in one tab, ChatGPT in another, files in Google
Drive, SOPs in Notion, frameworks in your head. Repeating yourself constantly. Copy-pasting
context between chats. Losing decisions from two weeks ago because the conversation disappeared.

The core problem: "AI tools don't know what a client is. They know what a user is. They know
what a chat is. But they don't understand that a business has clients, and each client has
their own world."

## The onboarding experience

The onboarding is the product demo — this is what converts people who see it.

**Flow:**
1. First 60 seconds: Simple questions. Name, business, industry, team size. Quick answers,
   one at a time. Like setting up a new iPhone.
2. Next 5-7 minutes: The onboarding interview. The platform speaks out loud (voice) and asks
   about the user's story — how they got here, what they do, what their methodology is, what
   holds them back. Not a form. A conversation.
3. The moment: The platform synthesizes everything into a profile. A "this is who you are"
   page. Written to show the user that the platform understands them — not surface level, but
   specifically, deeply them.
4. After that: No blank screen. The platform sends a personalized message, asks a question,
   guides to first action.

**Why it matters in copy:** The platform doesn't describe what it knows about you. It shows
you. That is what closes people — not the feature list.

## Features — what is actually built

Use this list for accuracy. Do not claim features not listed here.

**Core platform:**
- Three-layer architecture (Brain, Intelligence Mode, Workspaces)
- Voice-first interaction (Whisper transcription + Grok voice TTS)
- Executive Mode pipeline (every message goes straight to Claude Opus)
- 27 Intelligence tools for cross-client operations
- 16 action/output skills
- Streaming responses with markdown rendering
- Deep Research (Perplexity integration)
- Brain Dump mode (voice-first knowledge capture)
- Save as Document, Context, or Framework from chat
- Session history with retry

**Knowledge system:**
- Master Frameworks library
- Account Context (always-on knowledge)
- Instructions (pushed to workspaces automatically)
- Memory Facts (auto-extracted from conversations)
- Per-workspace Frameworks, Context, and Documents
- Folder system with inline TipTap editor
- Bulk import: Google Drive, Notion, GitHub, direct upload
- Two-phase document processing (instant save + background AI analysis)
- Hybrid search with Cohere reranking

**Client management:**
- Client creation, grid/list views
- 80+ field client profiles
- Projects with task management
- Workflows with templates
- Drag-and-drop, favorites, sorting, tags

**Integrations:**
- Google Drive, Google Calendar, Gmail
- Notion, Slack, GitHub, Zoom
- Circle SSO (community)

**Security:**
- Full data isolation between workspaces
- Row-level security on every table
- Encrypted data at rest
- Never used to train external models
