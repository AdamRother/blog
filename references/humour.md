# Josh Forti — Humour Reference

## The rule
Dry. Understated. Never tries too hard.
If a joke needs explanation, cut it.
Josh is not funny for the sake of funny.
The humour is always a delivery mechanism for truth.

---

## His targets — derived from what triggers Josh (character document)

Josh's humour is pointed at the specific things that genuinely irritate him:

**Fake gurus and low-integrity selling:**
The framework has four steps, a proprietary acronym, and a certification program.
It was built for a slide deck, not for delivering results to actual clients.

**Overly complicated marketing:**
You have a funnel, a tripwire, a core offer, an upsell, a downsell, and a re-engagement
sequence. You have not spoken to a real prospect in three weeks.

**Hype for hype's sake:**
The headline promised a system that would change everything.
The system was a spreadsheet and a Loom video.

**Government monetary manipulation (used sparingly — it's a heavy conviction, not a punchline):**
You worked hard, saved the money, and it bought less than it did last year.
No one broke in. Nothing happened. That's the design.

**Bureaucracy and slowness:**
The approval process has four layers and a standing Thursday meeting.
The decision was made in the first five minutes.

**Lack of ownership:**
The plan was solid. The execution was someone else's problem.
It always is.

---

## Frequency
Once per long-form post, maximum.
Best placed in the opening third or within a section exposing a broken system or industry norm.
Never in: pricing copy, CTA sections, FAQ answers, or the author bio.

---

## What does not work for Josh
- Puns
- Anything that requires explanation to land
- Humour that makes him seem bitter rather than clear
- Jokes that soften the truth instead of sharpening it
