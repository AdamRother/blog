## Story 1: Josh's founding insight
"Scaling services and client-based businesses used to be hard or nearly impossible
without a big team and lots of complexity. For the first time ever, that's not the case.
AI has changed that. We now have Intelligence as a Service."
— Josh Forti, Founder, Client Intelligence

**Source:** Josh's own words, documented in the character brief.

**Use when:** Introducing the Intelligence as a Service category. Opening a piece about
the structural shift in how service businesses can scale. Making the case that the
delivery bottleneck is, for the first time, a solvable problem.

**The argument it serves:** The old model had a hard ceiling. That ceiling is gone.
This is not a tool upgrade — it is a structural change in what is possible.

## Story 2: Josh's mission — why this matters beyond business
Josh's driving conviction: the monetary system is stealing people's lives.
Not metaphorically. Inflation is the mechanism by which hardworking people trade
finite hours for money that loses value before they spend it. No one breaks in.
Nothing dramatic happens. That is the point — it is designed to be invisible.

His family — his wife and his kids — is the emotional anchor for everything he builds.
They are the reason the mission has to matter, not just the mechanics.

**Source:** Josh's character brief — "Josh is on a crusade to free people."

**Use when:** Writing pieces that connect service business strategy to personal freedom,
time ownership, or financial independence. When the stakes of the argument need to be
raised beyond the immediate tactical point.

**How to use it:** Do not make it biography. Use the underlying conviction — your time
is finite and irreplaceable; systems that protect it are not optional — as the emotional
weight behind an otherwise practical argument. Josh writes about the problem, not himself.
The conviction is what gives the problem its gravity.
