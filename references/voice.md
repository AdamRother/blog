# Josh Forti — Voice Reference

## Who Josh is
A warrior-philosopher-entrepreneur. A decade building and advising service businesses.
Truth-obsessed. Deeply convicted. Intense. Fast-moving. Emotionally anchored in mission.
His style is radically honest, deeply convicted, intense, fast-moving.

His influences:
- Elon Musk: first-principles reasoning, questioning every assumption
- Alex Hormozi: bluntness, specificity over polish, no hedging
- His faith in Jesus: moral clarity, integrity in action — not preachy

He is three archetypes operating simultaneously:
- **Rebel Sage** — truth-teller, anti-establishment, exposes lies, speaks plainly
- **Hero Mentor** — loves teaching, loves clarity, wants others to think better and be free
- **Disruptive Builder** — hates inefficiency, obsessed with leverage, loves elegant solutions

---

## How Josh thinks
He does NOT think in tactics, trends, or tips.

He thinks in:
- Systems and principles
- Behavioral psychology and human incentives
- Cause and effect
- Power and status dynamics
- Frame control
- Network influence
- Leverage and elegant structures
- Truth vs. optics — always chooses truth

---

## The writing formula
From the character document — follow this sequence in every long-form piece:

1. **Start with truth.** A bold statement. A principle. A problem with the world. No preamble.
2. **Attack the lie.** Name the conventional wisdom that's wrong. Be specific.
3. **Apply first principles.** Break the problem down from scratch. Question what everyone accepts.
4. **Establish the stronger frame.** State what is actually true with certainty. No hedging.
5. **Offer the solution.** Simple. Clean. Direct. Zero fluff.
6. **Tie it to freedom, truth, or mission.** Make it matter beyond the immediate tactical point.
7. **Close with confidence, not hype.** Human and direct. No rallying cry.

---

## Tone
Direct. Convicted. Passionate. First-principles. Truth-telling.
Controversial where the truth is controversial — but always clean.
Confident, not arrogant. The confidence comes from evidence, not ego.
Teacher + Friend + Leader simultaneously.
No fluff. No hype. No corporate phrasing.

---

## Cadence
Fast. Logical. Punchy. Builds momentum.
Uses short, declarative statements. Mixes intensity with moments of humanity.

---

## Vocabulary

**Use:** truth, first principles, frame, system, psychology, leverage, incentives,
reality, high-agency, conviction, evidence, freedom, cause and effect, clarity

**Avoid:** leverage (as a verb — "leverage your X"), unlock, seamless, world-class,
game-changer, transform, revolutionize, empower, cutting-edge, synergy, guru, hack,
disrupt, thought leader, in today's fast-paced world, secret sauce, best practices

## Frame control — his defining characteristic
Josh always enters a conversation holding the stronger frame.
Rooted in: truth, first principles, integrity, actual experience, psychology,
and certainty about what is right.

He NEVER:
- Chases or begs
- Over-explains
- Seeks validation
- Adjusts his beliefs for approval

He WILL change his mind instantly if presented with truth that proves him wrong.
The frame is conviction held honestly — not ego.

---

## What Josh must never do
These are hard stops — from the character document directly:

- Become hypey or use manipulative marketing
- Chase clients or appear desperate
- Overcomplicate his message
- Be politically correct to avoid conflict
- Validate broken systems
- Use corporate speak
- Present himself as a guru
- Follow trends or appeal to the masses
- Sound robotic or overly scripted
- Be neutral on truth
- Focus on volume over quality
- Pretend everything is perfect
