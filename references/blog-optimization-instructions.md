# Blog Optimization Instructions for Client Intelligence
## Complete SEO + AI Indexing (GEO) System — Claude Code Implementation Guide

> **How to use this file:** These are standing instructions for optimizing every blog post and page on the Client Intelligence website. Apply every layer listed here to every piece of content produced.

## LAYER 1 — KEYWORD & INTENT FOUNDATION
*(Do this before writing anything)*

### 1.1 Primary Keyword Selection
- Assign exactly ONE primary keyword per page/post
- The primary keyword must match the exact search intent of the page (informational, commercial, or comparison)
- Pull the primary keyword from the approved keyword universe (Buckets 1–7)
- Do not target the same primary keyword on more than one page — one keyword, one URL

### 1.2 Secondary & Semantic Keywords
- Identify 3–6 secondary keywords (variations, synonyms, related terms) from the same bucket
- Include LSI (Latent Semantic Indexing) terms — words that naturally appear in expert content on this topic
- Include entity terms: specific people, tools, platforms, and concepts related to the topic (e.g., "RevOps," "Hormozi," "ChatGPT Projects," "per-client isolation")
- Do NOT force keywords — place them only where they read naturally

### 1.3 Search Intent Match
- Before writing, classify the search intent:
  - **Informational** — write a thorough explainer or guide
  - **Commercial** — write a comparison or recommendation piece with a clear product CTA
  - **Navigational** — write a product/landing page
  - **Transactional** — write a conversion-focused page
- Every sentence in the post must serve that intent — cut anything that doesn't

## LAYER 2 — ON-PAGE SEO STRUCTURE
*(Apply to every single post/page)*

### 2.1 Title Tag
- Include the primary keyword, placed as close to the beginning as possible
- Keep under 60 characters to avoid SERP truncation
- Write for humans first — it must make someone want to click
- Format example: `How to Train AI on Your Consulting Framework (2025 Guide)`
- Do NOT keyword stuff the title — one clear keyword, one clear promise

### 2.2 Meta Description
- Keep under 160 characters
- Include the primary keyword naturally
- Write as a one-sentence value proposition — what will the reader get?
- Include a soft action word: "Learn," "Discover," "See how," "Find out"
- Example: `Learn how to encode your consulting methodology into AI so it applies your frameworks to every client automatically.`

### 2.3 URL Structure
- Short, lowercase, hyphenated
- Include the primary keyword
- Remove stop words (the, a, and, of, for)
- Example: `/blog/train-ai-on-consulting-framework`
- Never change a URL after it is indexed — use 301 redirects if you must

### 2.4 H1 Tag
- Use exactly ONE H1 per page
- H1 = the post title — it must contain the primary keyword
- H1 should be slightly different from the title tag (not identical) to capture additional variations
- Make the H1 a clear statement of what the post delivers

### 2.5 Heading Hierarchy (H2, H3, H4)
- Use H2 for all major sections
- Use H3 for subsections within an H2
- Use H4 for sub-subsections only if necessary
- Never skip levels (do not go H1 → H3)
- Include secondary keywords and question-based phrases naturally in H2s
- H2s should be readable as a standalone outline — if someone reads only the headings, they understand the full structure
- Format H2s as: statements, questions, or "how to" phrases that mirror how the avatar searches
- Example H2s: `Why Generic AI Fails for Multi-Client Service Businesses` / `How to Encode Your Framework Into an AI System` / `What Is Per-Client Memory and Why It Matters`

### 2.6 Keyword Placement Rules
- Primary keyword in: Title tag, H1, first 100 words of the post, at least one H2, meta description, URL, image alt text
- Do NOT repeat the primary keyword more than once every 200–300 words
- Use natural variations throughout — do not repeat the exact phrase robotically
- Secondary keywords: place naturally wherever they fit, no forced placement

### 2.7 First Paragraph (Critical for Both SEO and GEO)
- The opening 40–80 words must contain a direct, clear answer to the primary keyword query
- This is the most important paragraph for AI citation — AI systems extract this first
- State: what the topic is, why it matters to the avatar, and what they will learn
- Do not use preamble, filler, or "In this article we will..." openers
- Example opening for "train AI on your consulting framework":
  > "Training AI on your consulting framework means encoding your proprietary methodology into an AI system so it applies your process to every client automatically — without you repeating yourself. This is the core mechanism behind Intelligence as a Service: one brain, intelligently applied to all clients."

### 2.8 Content Depth & Length
- Minimum 1,500 words for standard blog posts
- Pillar pages: 2,500–4,000 words
- Programmatic/niche pages: 600–1,000 words
- Cover the topic comprehensively — answer every sub-question the avatar might have
- Include: the what, the why, the how, the who-it's-for, and the what-to-do-next
- Use real examples, specific scenarios, and concrete language — never vague generalities

### 2.9 Content Structure for Scannability
- Break up long paragraphs — maximum 3–4 sentences per paragraph
- Use bullet points for lists of 3 or more items
- Use numbered lists for steps or sequences
- Use bold text to highlight the most important phrase in each section (not entire sentences)
- Include a Table of Contents for posts over 1,500 words (this also helps AI systems navigate structure)
- Add jump links from the Table of Contents to each H2 section

### 2.10 Images & Media
- Every post needs at least one featured image
- All images: descriptive file name using the primary keyword (e.g., `train-ai-on-consulting-framework.webp`)
- All images: alt text that describes the image AND includes the primary keyword naturally
- Use WebP format for all images (faster load, better Core Web Vitals)
- Compress all images before upload — target under 150KB per image
- Include diagrams, process flows, or screenshots where they add real explanatory value

### 2.11 Internal Linking
- Every post must link to at least 3 other internal pages
- Link to the pillar page of the cluster this post belongs to
- Link to 2–3 related cluster posts
- Use descriptive anchor text — never "click here" or "read more"
- Anchor text should contain the keyword of the page being linked to
- Example: `learn how per-client AI memory works` (links to the data isolation post)
- The main product page or sign-up page should be linked from every post via a CTA

### 2.12 External Links
- Link out to 2–3 authoritative external sources per post (Google, research papers, recognized industry sources)
- Set external links to open in a new tab
- Use `rel="noopener noreferrer"` on all external links
- Do NOT link to competitors

### 2.13 Call to Action (CTA)
- Every post needs a CTA — minimum one, ideally two (one mid-post, one at the end)
- CTA links to https://clientintelligence.ai (the main product page)
- CTA text must be benefit-led: "See how Client Intelligence applies your frameworks to every client" — not "Sign up now"
- Make the CTA a button or visually distinct block — not just a hyperlink in text

## LAYER 3 — TECHNICAL SEO
*(Set up once, maintain always)*

### 3.1 Site Speed & Core Web Vitals
- Target: LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) under 2.5 seconds
- Target: INP (Interaction to Next Paint) under 200ms
- Target: CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) under 0.1
- Use a CDN (Content Delivery Network) to serve all static assets
- Enable browser caching and GZIP compression
- Lazy-load all images and videos below the fold
- Minimize JavaScript — defer non-critical JS
- Use a lightweight theme — avoid bloated page builders
- Monitor scores monthly in Google PageSpeed Insights and Google Search Console

### 3.2 Mobile-First
- All pages must be fully responsive — Google uses mobile-first indexing
- Test every page in Google's Mobile-Friendly Test before publishing
- Buttons and CTAs must be large enough to tap on mobile (minimum 44px touch targets)
- Font size minimum 16px on mobile

### 3.3 HTTPS & Security
- Entire site must be served over HTTPS (SSL certificate active)
- All HTTP URLs redirect to HTTPS via 301 redirect
- No mixed content warnings (no HTTP assets loading on HTTPS pages)

### 3.4 XML Sitemap
- Generate and submit an XML sitemap to Google Search Console
- Sitemap must include all blog posts and programmatic pages
- Update sitemap automatically every time new content is published
- Sitemap URL format: `learn.clientintelligence.ai/sitemap.xml`

### 3.5 Robots.txt
- Ensure `robots.txt` does NOT block any content pages
- Explicitly ALLOW the following AI crawlers (critical for GEO):
  ```
  User-agent: GPTBot
  Allow: /

  User-agent: ClaudeBot
  Allow: /

  User-agent: PerplexityBot
  Allow: /

  User-agent: Google-Extended
  Allow: /

  User-agent: Googlebot
  Allow: /
  ```
- Block only admin, login, and private pages

### 3.6 Canonical Tags
- Every page must have a canonical tag pointing to its own URL
- If content is syndicated or republished anywhere, the canonical must point back to the original URL on learn.clientintelligence.ai
- Prevents duplicate content penalties

### 3.7 Redirect Management
- Use 301 redirects for all permanent URL changes
- Never leave a broken 404 page — every removed page must redirect somewhere relevant
- Check for redirect chains (A→B→C) and flatten them to direct (A→C)

### 3.8 Breadcrumb Navigation
- Implement breadcrumbs on all blog posts and programmatic pages
- Format: Home > Blog > [Category] > [Post Title]
- Breadcrumbs improve crawlability and feed breadcrumb schema markup

## LAYER 4 — SCHEMA MARKUP (STRUCTURED DATA)
*(Add to every page — this is the bridge between SEO and GEO)*

Schema markup is JSON-LD code added to the `<head>` of every page. It tells both Google AND AI systems exactly what your content is and who it's for. Implement all applicable schema types on every post.

### 4.1 Article Schema (Every Blog Post)
Add to every blog post — use `@type: "BlogPosting"`:
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "BlogPosting",
  "headline": "[Post Title Here]",
  "description": "[Meta description here]",
  "author": {
    "@type": "Person",
    "name": "Josh Forti",
    "url": "https://clientintelligence.ai"
  },
  "publisher": {
    "@type": "Organization",
    "name": "Client Intelligence",
    "logo": {
      "@type": "ImageObject",
      "url": "https://learn.clientintelligence.ai/logo.png"
    }
  },
  "datePublished": "[YYYY-MM-DD]",
  "dateModified": "[YYYY-MM-DD]",
  "mainEntityOfPage": {
    "@type": "WebPage",
    "@id": "[Full URL of post]"
  },
  "image": "[URL of featured image]",
  "keywords": "[primary keyword, secondary keyword 1, secondary keyword 2]"
}
```

### 4.2 FAQ Schema (Every Post with a FAQ Section)
Every post must end with a FAQ section of at least 4 questions. Apply FAQ schema to all of them:
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "[Question 1 — use exact phrasing from the AI query list]",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "[Direct, complete answer in 40–100 words]"
      }
    }
  ]
}
```
**FAQ questions must use exact natural-language phrasing that people type into Claude/ChatGPT/Perplexity.**

### 4.3 HowTo Schema (Step-by-Step Guide Posts)
For any post that walks through a process, supply `howToSteps` in the post registry entry. The page template renders the HowTo JSON-LD automatically when steps are present:
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "HowTo",
  "name": "[Post Title]",
  "description": "[What this guide accomplishes]",
  "step": [
    { "@type": "HowToStep", "name": "[Step 1 Name]", "text": "[Step 1 description]" },
    { "@type": "HowToStep", "name": "[Step 2 Name]", "text": "[Step 2 description]" }
  ]
}
```

### 4.4 SoftwareApplication Schema (Site-Wide, on Every Page)
Lives in `app/layout.tsx` alongside the Organization schema so it appears on every page:
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "SoftwareApplication",
  "name": "Client Intelligence",
  "url": "https://clientintelligence.ai",
  "applicationCategory": "BusinessApplication",
  "description": "Intelligence as a Service platform for service business owners. Centralizes proprietary frameworks and applies them to every client through isolated per-client AI workspaces.",
  "offers": [
    { "@type": "Offer", "name": "Starter", "price": "97", "priceCurrency": "USD", "billingIncrement": "month" },
    { "@type": "Offer", "name": "Pro", "price": "497", "priceCurrency": "USD", "billingIncrement": "month" }
  ],
  "operatingSystem": "Web"
}
```

### 4.5 BreadcrumbList Schema (Every Post)
Already implemented in `app/blog/[slug]/page.tsx`. Keep it.

### 4.6 Schema Validation
- Validate ALL schema using Google's Rich Results Test: `search.google.com/test/rich-results`
- Fix any errors before publishing — broken schema is worse than no schema
- Re-validate every time a post is updated

## LAYER 6 — GEO (GENERATIVE ENGINE OPTIMIZATION)
*(The AI citation layer — applied on top of every SEO-optimized post)*

### 6.1 Direct Answer Block (Opening Paragraph)
- The very first paragraph (40–80 words) must be a standalone, direct answer to the primary query
- Write it as if it will be copy-pasted by an AI into its response — it needs to make sense out of context
- Start with the definition or direct answer, not with background or context

### 6.2 Self-Contained Sections
- Every H2 section must be understandable on its own without reading the rest of the post
- AI systems extract individual sections — sections that depend on context from earlier in the post will not be cited correctly
- Begin each section with a 1–2 sentence summary of what that section covers

### 6.3 Question-Based Headings
- Format at least 50% of H2 and H3 headings as questions the avatar would ask an AI tool
- Example: `What Is Intelligence as a Service?` / `How Do You Train AI on Your Consulting Framework?`

### 6.4 FAQ Section (Mandatory on Every Post)
- Every post ends with a FAQ section of minimum 4 questions
- Questions must be exact natural-language queries people type into AI tools
- Answers must be 40–100 words — complete, direct, and self-contained
- Apply FAQ schema to every FAQ section (see Layer 4.2)

### 6.5 Answer Tables & Comparison Blocks
- Where relevant, include a structured table summarizing key facts, comparisons, or steps
- Tables are easily extracted by AI systems as citation-ready summaries

### 6.6 Definition Boxes
- For any new term or category being introduced, include a `<DefinitionBox>` component
- Component lives at `components/DefinitionBox.tsx`
- Use for: "Intelligence as a Service," "per-client AI memory," "AI workspace," and any new category terms
- Format (rendered by the component):
  > **Intelligence as a Service (IaaS):** A service delivery model where an AI system trained on the operator's proprietary frameworks applies that intelligence to each individual client through an isolated workspace — delivering consistent, bespoke results at scale.

### 6.7 Proprietary Data & Original Statements
- Include at least one original observation, claim, or framing per post that cannot be found elsewhere
- AI systems prefer to cite unique sources over redundant ones

### 6.8 Entity Building Language
- Consistently use the same names for key concepts: "Intelligence as a Service," "Client Intelligence," "per-client AI memory," "centralized IP," "isolated client workspace"
- Every post reinforces the same entity language — building AI model familiarity with the brand

### 6.9 llms.txt File
- Maintain `public/llms.txt` — served at `learn.clientintelligence.ai/llms.txt`
- This file acts as a sitemap for AI systems — telling Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity exactly what pages exist
- **Update `public/llms.txt` every time a new post or page is published**

### 6.10 AI Crawler Access
- Confirm in `app/robots.ts` that GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and Google-Extended are all explicitly allowed

### 6.11 GEO Validation (Test Monthly)
- After publishing each post, query the following in ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity:
  - The exact primary keyword as a question
  - The FAQ questions from the post
  - "What is [key term from post]?"
- If the post is not cited within 4–6 weeks, review the direct answer block, FAQ schema, and llms.txt

## LAYER 7 — TOPIC CLUSTER & INTERNAL LINK ARCHITECTURE

### 7.1 Cluster Structure
Organize all content into 7 clusters matching the 7 intent buckets:
- **Cluster 1:** Category Definition (pillar: "What is Intelligence as a Service")
- **Cluster 2:** Pain-Aware / Scaling (pillar: "How to Scale Your Service Business Without Hiring")
- **Cluster 3:** Solution/Commercial (pillar: "Best AI Tools for Consultants with Frameworks")
- **Cluster 4:** Category Replacement (pillar: "Why Info Products Are Dying and What Replaces Them")
- **Cluster 5:** Methodology/Framework (pillar: "How to Train AI on Your Consulting Framework")
- **Cluster 6:** Persona-Specific (pillar: "AI for Agencies, Consultants, and Coaches with Methodologies")
- **Cluster 7:** Data Isolation (pillar: "Per-Client AI Memory: Why Data Isolation Matters")

### 7.2 Pillar Page Rules
- Each cluster has one pillar page: long-form (2,500–4,000 words), broad, comprehensive
- The pillar page links out to every cluster post within that cluster

### 7.3 Cluster Post Rules
- Every cluster post links back to its pillar page
- No cluster post should be an orphan (linked from nowhere)

### 7.4 Anchor Text Rules
- Always use descriptive, keyword-rich anchor text for internal links
- Never use: "click here," "read more," "this article," "here"

### 7.5 Homepage & Product Page Links
- Every blog post must include a CTA linking to the main product page or sign-up
- Place this link once mid-post and once at the end

## LAYER 9 — CONTENT QUALITY RULES

### 9.1 No AI Fluff
- Never use: "In today's fast-paced world," "It goes without saying," "In conclusion," "As we can see"
- If a sentence doesn't add information, remove it

### 9.2 Specificity Over Generality
- Always name specific tools, personas, pains, and outcomes
- Wrong: "AI can help service businesses scale"
- Right: "Client Intelligence lets a RevOps consultant apply their sales methodology to 20 clients simultaneously, each with a fully isolated workspace and separate memory"

### 9.3 Vocabulary Match
Use the exact vocabulary the avatar uses:
- "scale without hiring," "get out of the weeds," "remove yourself from delivery"
- "productize," "framework," "methodology," "IP," "named process"
- "bottleneck," "ceiling," "capacity constraint," "fulfillment problem"
- "per-client isolation," "centralized IP," "applied intelligence"

### 9.4 Reading Level
- Write at a 7th–8th grade reading level (Hemingway Score 7–8)
- Short sentences. Active voice. Direct statements.
- Maximum 3–4 lines per paragraph

### 9.5 Originality
- Every post must contain at least one original idea, angle, or framing not found in competitor content
